# Prefix Guide

Detailed criteria, signal phrases, and worked examples for each of the nine prefixes. Read when classification is non-obvious. Examples drawn from Rocky's actual chat history May 2026.

## EXN — Expert Network

**What it is.** Anything where Rocky is interacting with an expert network (GLG, Guidepoint, Dialectica, AlphaSights, Mosaic, Vantage Point, third-party screening, expert-call brokers) — screening forms, call prep, post-call notes, briefings.

**Signal phrases in the chat.**
- "screener" / "screening" / "screening form"
- "expert call" / "expert network"
- "GLG" / "Guidepoint" / "Dialectica" / similar
- "screening questionnaire"
- Q1/Q2/Q3 form-fill pattern with multiple-choice options
- "call ID" / "session reference" / "tip" / "engagement code"

**Worked examples.**
- `EXN | AI Industry Advisor Screener | 2026-05-18`
- `EXN | GLG EaaS Outcome Models | 2026-05-18`
- `EXN | On-Demand Mfg Platforms - Merged Intelligence Brief | 2026-05-11 [DOC]`
- `EXN | Atlas Copco 1Q26 Earnings Guidepoint | 2026-05-11`

**What it is NOT.**
- General industry intelligence Rocky did for his own knowledge → READ
- Investment research for his own portfolio → LIFE
- Competitive intel for a paying client → CLIENT

**Edge case: "Finding Project Prometheus Chats" type entries.** Looking for prior expert-call material is admin, not screening. Still acceptable under EXN if the goal is preparing for a future EXN engagement; otherwise consider it the prefix of whatever the upstream task is.

## KAI — Kaipability

**What it is.** Anything about the firm itself — strategy, positioning, offerings, founding principles, fundraising, internal architecture, the firm's intellectual frameworks (AIRL, MCRL, the Five Matterings), website design strategy, marketing strategy, partnership pitches where Kaipability is the entity being positioned.

**Signal phrases.**
- "Kaipability" / "the firm" / "the business"
- "offering" / "positioning" / "founding principles"
- "ARIA Activation Partner" / "ARIA pitch" (when Kaipability is the bidder)
- "fundraising" / "LP memorandum" / "investor"
- "AIRL" / "MCRL" / "Five Matterings" (framework development)

**Worked examples.**
- `KAI | ARIA Activation Partner Pitch | 2026-05-18 [DOC]`
- `KAI | Offering Proposal | 2026-05-18 [DOC]`
- `KAI | Founding Principles - Lenny Podcast Themes | 2026-05-12`
- `KAI | Evolution Advisory to Capability Institution | 2026-05-13`
- `KAI | Ontologies & Knowledge Graphs for Kaipability | 2026-05-19 [>]`

**What it is NOT.**
- A specific paid client engagement → CLIENT
- Rocky's personal career trajectory or role pursuit → CAR
- Article-form thinking about the firm's themes → ART (because the output is the article, not the strategy)

**Tiebreaker note.** A Kaipability pitch to a named potential client (e.g. ARIA, Saab) is KAI if it's about positioning and the deliverable is internal thinking. It's CLIENT if the deliverable is a quotation, proposal document, or formal tender response intended to be sent.

## CLIENT — Client work

**What it is.** Work tied to a named client with money on the table (paid, paying, or active commercial pipeline). The dominant deliverable goes to or supports a specific external entity, not Kaipability internally.

**Signal phrases.**
- Named client: "Saab", "Wärtsilä", "Atlas Copco" (in a paid-engagement context), "GoDigital" SME client name
- "quote" / "quotation" / "proposal" / "tender" / "SOW"
- "client wants" / "for the client" / "deliverable for"
- Tender notice numbers (e.g. "043410-2026")
- Reference codes from the quoting skill (e.g. `00-SAAB1001MRV1`)

**Worked examples.**
- `CLIENT | Saab Phase 1 Scoping Quote | 2026-04-27 [DOC]`
- `CLIENT | Atlas Copco Deployment Readiness Quote Revision | 2026-05-14`
- `CLIENT | UK Gov Tender 043410-2026 | 2026-05-13`
- `CLIENT | KaiTrack Quoting System Architecture | 2026-05-18 [>]` (tool work that is part of a client engagement infrastructure)
- `CLIENT | Atlas Copco M&A Advisory Brief | 2026-05-13 [DOC]`

**What it is NOT.**
- Industry intelligence about a former employer (e.g. analysing Edwards/Atlas Copco from the outside) → READ or EXN depending on context
- Generic positioning chat referring to clients abstractly → KAI
- Building Kaipability's own internal tooling (even if it serves clients) → TOOL or KAI

**Edge case: ex-employer analysis.** Analysing Edwards/Atlas Copco when no commercial relationship is active is READ (industry intelligence) or EXN (if expert-network framed). It's CLIENT only when there's a current commercial relationship and Atlas Copco is the buyer.

## ART — Article / Writing

**What it is.** Long-form publishable writing — Substack articles, op-eds, response pieces, analytical essays. The work product is text intended for publication, whether or not it has been published yet.

**Signal phrases.**
- "Substack" / "substack draft" / "article"
- "publish" / "for publication" / "response piece"
- Version numbers attached to drafts (v2, v3, v5)
- Title-and-subtitle format being workshopped
- Analytical writing skill being invoked
- Mention of "Lynel mode" / "centaur" / "killshot" / "pull quote"

**Worked examples.**
- `ART | Substack Article - Patents & Modern Industrialists | 2026-05-18 [DOC]`
- `ART | Manufacturing Doomerism Report | 2026-05-17 [DOC]`
- `ART | UMIST Article Supermarket Sweep | 2026-05-15`
- `ART | Dwarkesh Blog Prize Response + HTML | 2026-05-13 [DOC]`
- `ART | Substack - Manufacturing as Noun v5 | 2026-05-11 [DOC]`

**What it is NOT.**
- A LinkedIn comment or short post (≤300 words) → LI
- Reading and reacting to an article without producing a published response → READ
- Marketing post tied to an article release → LI (the marketing post is the output) or ART (the article is the primary work)

**Tiebreaker.** If the session produced both an article AND a marketing post, use ART (the article is the bigger lift). The LinkedIn post is implied as a downstream artifact.

**Version note.** If the chat is workshopping a known version of an article, include the version: `ART | Manufacturing Fetishism v5 | 2026-04-02 [DOC]`. Versions help when several chats touch the same piece.

## LI — LinkedIn

**What it is.** Public-facing LinkedIn output. Comments on other people's posts, principle-of-the-day posts, micro-content, screengrab-in / draft-comment-out workflow. The output is visible to a wider audience, not a private DM to a named individual.

**Signal phrases.**
- "LinkedIn" / "LI post" / "comment"
- Screenshot or pasted post from LinkedIn
- "principle" / "principle post" / "weekly post"
- Kaipability marketing skill invoked
- "do a comment" / "should I comment or post"
- "respond to this post"

**Worked examples.**
- `LI | Comment vs Post - Jalal Semiconductor Panel | 2026-05-18`
- `LI | Comment - Eric Schmidt Commencement | 2026-05-18`
- `LI | Custom Hashtag Strategy | 2026-05-14`
- `LI | Kaipability Page Growth Strategy | 2026-05-12`
- `LI | Comment - Drucker Quote Inversion Tangen Post | 2026-05-11`

**What it is NOT.**
- A full Substack article → ART
- A private LinkedIn DM or direct message to a named individual → COMS
- Pure analysis of someone's post without drafting a public reply → READ
- Strategic positioning thinking that touches LinkedIn but isn't drafting content → KAI

## COMS — Communications

**What it is.** Drafting a private message — email, DM, text, Slack, WhatsApp — to a named recipient. The work product is the sent or near-sent message itself, not the analysis behind it. Non-commercial-yet (no quote, no contract, no tender) and non-role-pursuit (not a cover letter, not a recruiter reply, not a fellowship pitch).

**Signal phrases.**
- "draft an email" / "reply to [name]" / "what should I say to [name]"
- "write a message" / "respond to this"
- `message_compose` tool invoked
- Named recipient at the top (Hi John, Dear Vicente, Hey Tom)
- The chat ends with a finalised message Rocky will paste into email/DM

**Worked examples.**
- `COMS | Email Draft - John Mfg Technologist | 2026-05-19`
- `COMS | Email Draft - Vicente Orts Bessemer | 2026-03-31`
- `COMS | LinkedIn DM - Tom Kalil Philanthropy 2.0 | 2026-04-03`
- `COMS | Email to Peter Winton - Article Review | 2026-04-02`

**What it is NOT.**
- Email about a quote, tender, or contract → CLIENT
- Cover letter, recruiter reply, fellowship application → CAR
- Public LinkedIn comment or post → LI
- Chat that workshops firm strategy and produces an email as a side artifact → KAI

**Tiebreaker note.** The hardest call is COMS vs KAI. Test: if you took the email away, would the chat still feel like a substantive deliverable? Yes → KAI (the thinking was the work; the email was a tail). No → COMS (the message was the work; the thinking was the means).

**Tiebreaker note (CLIENT).** The moment money or a commercial pipeline enters the email (quote, scope, tender), reclassify as CLIENT. COMS is for the pre-commercial relationship-building tier, even if the recipient might one day become a client.

**Watch this prefix.** COMS was added in the v2 audit (May 2026) after the first 30-day review surfaced ~3-5% of chats forced into KAI or CAR. Expected steady-state share: 3-7%. Below 3% for three months → consider folding back into KAI/CAR; above 10% and the boundary with KAI might need re-examining (some KAI sessions are being collapsed into their email output).

## READ — Reading & Reacting

**What it is.** Rocky pastes someone else's content (article, report, post, podcast transcript, event recap) for analysis. No publishable output is produced — the work product stays internal.

**Signal phrases.**
- Pasted article text, URL, or PDF for analysis
- "thoughts?" / "what do you think" / "read this"
- "fact check this"
- Event recap, panel debrief, conference summary
- "FT article" / "Economist piece" / "this report"

**Worked examples.**
- `READ | Matta AI in Manufacturing Article | 2026-05-18`
- `READ | MIT Panel AI & Jobs - John Werner | 2026-05-18`
- `READ | Energy Systems Catapult Closure | 2026-05-17`
- `READ | Henry Royce Institute Leadership Issue | 2026-05-17`
- `READ | FT Article - Family Firms Less Intelligent | 2026-05-11`

**What it is NOT.**
- Reading something then writing a published response → ART
- Reading something then drafting a LinkedIn comment → LI
- Reading something for an expert-network call prep → EXN

**Watch this prefix.** READ is the most likely to become a dustbin. If it grows past ~30% of total chats, split it into READ-ART (precursor reading for articles) and READ-INTEL (industry/competitive intelligence). For now, one prefix is enough.

## CAR — Career & Personal Positioning

**What it is.** Decisions about Rocky personally — roles he's pursuing, career trajectory, perception in the market, CV work, professional brand. The subject is "Rocky-the-individual" not "Kaipability-the-firm".

**Signal phrases.**
- "career" / "trajectory" / "perception"
- Named role pursuit: "CTrO", "ARIA", "DSIT", "ARCA" (when Rocky is the candidate)
- "should I take" / "should I apply" / "should I pursue"
- CV comparison or critique
- Personal positioning vs another named person
- Mentor conversations about Rocky's path

**Worked examples.**
- `CAR | Career Trajectory Options | 2026-05-18`
- `CAR | CTrO Role - DSIT Pursuit | 2026-05-11`
- `CAR | ARIA CTrO Position Strategy | 2026-04-27`

**What it is NOT.**
- Kaipability's firm-level positioning → KAI
- A career-coaching style chat where the subject is someone else → READ or LIFE

**Tiebreaker note.** When Rocky is pursuing a role THROUGH Kaipability (e.g. positioning Kaipability as an ARIA Activation Partner), it's KAI not CAR. CAR is for Rocky personally being the candidate.

## TOOL — Build / Technical

**What it is.** The work product is code, an HTML page, an embed, a script, a skill, a video prompt, a website page, a Carrd block, a Veo prompt, a piece of software. Something Claude or Rocky can run or deploy.

**Signal phrases.**
- Code blocks in the output
- "build me" / "create a page" / "write a script"
- "Veo prompt" / "video gen" / "Gemini"
- "Carrd" / "embed" / "HTML"
- "skill" (when building a skill, not running one)
- KaiTrack, RockyOS infrastructure work
- Website iteration

**Worked examples.**
- `TOOL | Veo Video Prompt - Pipe Explosion | 2026-05-11`
- `TOOL | Kaipability Website Accessibility Check | 2026-05-12`
- `TOOL | Sunday Times Access for PhD Research | 2026-05-11`
- `TOOL | Chat Naming Skill | 2026-05-19 [DOC]` (this one)

**What it is NOT.**
- A chat where Claude built an HTML diagram as a side-output of a strategy conversation → use the strategy's prefix (KAI / READ / CAR), with [DOC] flag if the HTML was exported
- Skill creation done as part of a Kaipability strategy session → KAI
- Building a quote document — that's CLIENT (it's a deliverable to a client, not a tool)

**Watch this prefix.** TOOL should be earning its place. If after six months it has fewer than 20 entries total, fold it into the dominant adjacent prefix and remove TOOL.

## LIFE — Personal / Admin

**What it is.** Non-work. Family, travel, household, personal finance, shopping, kids' content, personal investments, consumer disputes.

**Signal phrases.**
- Travel planning, holiday research, restaurant booking
- Children's content, age-appropriateness questions
- Household admin, shopping, spec comparison
- Personal investment research (not work-related industry analysis)
- Consumer disputes (Virgin Media, energy providers, etc.)
- Personal grocery / household / shower / consumer-product spec
- Rebecca / family member named in personal context

**Worked examples.**
- `LIFE | Pre-Depression Investment Priorities | 2026-05-17`
- `LIFE | Grocery List Price Check | 2026-05-16`
- `LIFE | Bristan Thermostatic Shower Spec | 2026-05-13`
- `LIFE | Kaipability Cash Deposit Options | 2026-05-11` (personal finance even if Kaipability-named)
- `LIFE | Corfu Bus Routes & Day Trips | 2026-04-09`

**What it is NOT.**
- Personal investment work that turned into a publishable article → ART
- Personal positioning vs a Kaipability question → KAI or CAR

**Tiebreaker.** Cash deposit options for the Kaipability business account are LIFE not KAI — they're personal finance decisions for Rocky-as-business-owner, not strategic firm positioning. The test: would Rocky tell a journalist about this work? If yes → KAI. If no → LIFE.
